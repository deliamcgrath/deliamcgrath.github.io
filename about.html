about

I’m currently an applied researcher at Konpanion, where I develop software for our autonomous pet robot. Our work draws on research in human-robot interaction, computational biology, cognitive AI, and game agent design. Rather than focusing solely on methods, we ask broader questions: How can our robot behave in ways similar to real animals — emotionally, cognitively, and socially? How does it interpret sensory input to infer human states? How can it form memories, make plans, and develop a personality shaped by its interactions?

Generally, my main interests lie within adaptation, interaction, and architectures. While you don't have to do what you love to be successful, I find that focusing here makes it more fun than it probably should be.
