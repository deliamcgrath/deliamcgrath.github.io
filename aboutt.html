about

I’m currently an applied researcher at Konpanion, where I develop software for our pet robot, focusing on perception, planning, and autonomy.

My main interests lie in architecture, adaptation, and multimodal models. I started out fascinated by low-level architecture (VHDL, Assembly), then grew interested in ML architecture (Python, C++). I think projects and experiences serve more as a compass guiding the direction of my interests rather than a fixed map. While you don’t have to do what you love to be successful, I’m lucky enough to always find myself working on projects I love—and honestly, it makes work way more fun than it probably should be.
